# Decorum - Game Rules

> A cooperative, hidden information game of passive aggressive cohabitation.

Decorum is designed by Charlie Mackin, Harry Mackin, and Drew Tenenbaum, published by [Floodgate Games](https://floodgate.games/products/decorum).

## Overview

Players share the same objective: decorate a house in a way that makes everyone happy. The problem is, different things make each player happy and nobody says exactly what they need. Players must work together to fulfill every condition on everyone's secret cards, with very limited communication.

- 2-player game: a campaign of 20 scenarios, played in order
- 3/4-player game: 10 standalone scenarios, played in any order

## The House

The house is a 2x2 grid of 4 rooms:

```
 Bathroom  |  Bedroom
-----------+-----------
Living Room|  Kitchen
```

In 3/4-player roommate scenarios, the upper-left room becomes a second bedroom (Bedroom A), and the upper-right remains Bedroom B.

Each room has 4 features that can be changed:
- **Wall color** (paint) - always has a color, no style
- **Lamp** slot - can be filled or empty
- **Curio** slot - can be filled or empty
- **Wall hanging** slot - can be filled or empty

### Spatial Terminology

| Term | Rooms |
|------|-------|
| Upstairs | Bathroom + Bedroom |
| Downstairs | Living Room + Kitchen |
| Left side | Bathroom + Living Room |
| Right side | Bedroom + Kitchen |

## Objects

There are 3 types of objects: **lamps**, **curios**, and **wall hangings**.

Each object has a **color** and a **style**. Not every combination exists - there are exactly **12 valid objects**:

| | Modern | Antique | Retro | Unusual |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wall Hanging** | Red | Green | Blue | Yellow |
| **Curio** | Green | Blue | Yellow | Red |
| **Lamp** | Blue | Yellow | Red | Green |

Each object type is available in all 4 colors and all 4 styles, but not every style is available in every color. For example, there are no "red antique" objects.

The game supply contains **4 copies of each of the 12 specific objects** (48 tokens total).

## Colors

There are 4 colors: **red**, **yellow**, **blue**, and **green**.

Colors are grouped by temperature:
- **Warm colors**: red, yellow
- **Cool colors**: blue, green

Both objects and wall paint come in these 4 colors. The term "feature" refers to both objects and wall colors.

## Styles

There are 4 styles (objects only, not wall paint): **modern**, **antique**, **retro**, and **unusual**.

## Scenarios

Each scenario includes:
- 1 **setup card** showing the initial house configuration
- **Conditions cards** (1 per player, kept secret) listing 3-5 conditions each
- Possibly surprises revealed in later scenarios

### Scenario Setup

1. Place wall color tokens matching the setup card
2. Place object tokens into the matching room slots (some may be empty)
3. Give each player their face-down conditions card (keep it secret)

## Gameplay

### Player Turns

Each round, every player takes a turn consisting of 3 steps:

**1. Perform an Action** (choose one):
- **Add an object**: Place an object from the supply into an empty slot
- **Remove an object**: Return an object from the house to the supply
- **Swap an object**: Replace an object with a different object of the same type (e.g., swap one curio for another curio). Cannot swap objects between rooms.
- **Paint a wall**: Change a room's wall color. Cannot swap colors between rooms.
- **Pass**: Do nothing (only allowed if all your conditions are already met)

Important: You cannot remove an object and add a different type on the same turn.

**2. Fulfillment Check**: If all conditions on your card are met, declare "I'm fulfilled." Note: fulfillment is checked after every action — another player's action can cause you to lose fulfillment.

**3. Comment from Partner**: Your partner responds to your action with a positive, negative, or neutral comment (e.g., "I love it!", "I hate it!", "Meh."). No other communication is allowed - no hints about specific conditions.

### Rounds

After both players take a turn, the round marker advances 1 space. The game has a maximum of 30 rounds.

### Heart-to-Heart (2-Player)

Three times during the game, players have a Heart-to-Heart. The first occurs at round 15. After the first Heart-to-Heart only, the round marker resets to 1. Subsequent Heart-to-Hearts occur at rounds 5 and 10 (on the reset track), with no further resets.

1. Each player shares 1 of their conditions aloud
2. Flip a heart token face-down and place it to the right of the round track

Players may agree to skip ahead to the first Heart-to-Heart early.

### House Meeting (3/4-Player)

Similar to Heart-to-Heart but occurs every 5 rounds (5 meetings total):
1. Everyone states how they feel about the house overall
2. Each player picks 1 housemate to share a small condition card with (kept secret from others)
3. Flip a heart token and reset the round marker

Additional 3/4-player changes:
- Comments are optional — any player who wants to may comment on any action (not just clockwise)
- **Roommate Swap** action available: Move your token to the other bedroom, choosing which occupant to kick out if the room has multiple. In 3-player games, you may also swap into a bedroom with only one occupant (yourself), kicking them to the other room.
- Conditions may refer to "your room" (the room with your roommate token)
- Each player has **big condition cards** (kept secret, like 2P) and **small condition cards** that are shared during House Meetings. Small cards can be taken back and re-shared with a different player at a later meeting.
- In 3-player games, the 4th player's small condition cards are distributed among the 3 players during setup.

## Ending the Game

The game ends in 1 of 2 ways:
1. All players are fulfilled (all conditions on all cards met) - you win!
2. After 30 rounds with no heart tokens remaining - time's up

## Scoring

- **3 points** for every condition met (across all players' cards)
- **If every condition was met**: also gain **2 points** per heart token remaining on the left side of the round track

Winning sooner gives a bigger bonus (more remaining hearts), but only if every condition is fulfilled.

Maximum scores:
- 2-player (10 conditions): 36 points
- 3/4-player (12 conditions): 46 points (12×3 + 5×2 = 46)

## Conditions

Each player receives 3-5 secret conditions. All conditions across all players must be met simultaneously to win. Conditions can reference:

- **Positive/negative**: "must contain" vs. "must not contain"
- **Specific rooms**: "The kitchen must contain a lamp"
- **Floors/sides**: "The upstairs must contain a lamp" or "The right side must not contain a curio"
- **Specific objects**: "The bathroom must contain a yellow antique lamp"
- **Wall colors**: "The downstairs must not contain any rooms painted green"
- **Object colors**: "The living room must contain a yellow object"
- **Feature colors** (objects and/or wall colors): "The upstairs must not contain the color blue"
- **Color temperature**: "The upstairs must not contain any warm colors"
- **Styles**: "The kitchen must contain an antique object"
- **Quantities**: "The house must contain at least 2 wall hangings"
- **Identical objects**: Two or more objects with the same color, type, and style
- **And/or**: "The kitchen must contain blue (as objects and/or wall color)"
- **Leftmost/rightmost object**: The object closest to the left/right wall of the room (ignoring empty slots)
- **Most/least common**: A trait that must appear more/less than any other trait of the same type
- **Inseparable features**: Two features that must not appear in a room without each other

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Object | Lamp, curio, or wall hanging |
| Empty slot | An object slot with nothing in it |
| Feature | Object or paint color |
| Warm colors | Red and yellow |
| Cool colors | Blue and green |
| Upstairs | Bathroom and bedroom |
| Downstairs | Kitchen and living room |
| Left side | Bathroom and living room |
| Right side | Kitchen and bedroom |
| Identical | Two or more objects with the same color, type, and style |
| Specific object | A fully qualified object, e.g., "unusual yellow wall hanging" |
| And/or | One thing, or the other, or both |
| Inseparable | Two features that must not appear in a room without each other |
| Most/least common | A trait appearing more/less than any other of its kind |
| Leftmost/rightmost | The object closest to that wall, ignoring empty slots |
